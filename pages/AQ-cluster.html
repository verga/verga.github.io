Lectures on advanced quantum mechanics

Cluster state and quantum computation

The most basic model of quantum computation is the circuit, an initial state, usually a product state \(\ket{0}^N\) of \(N\) qubits, is acted upon by a sequence of universal one and two qubits gates; for instance the pauli \(\bm \sigma = (X,Y,Z)\), hadamard \(\mathsf{H}\) and \(\pi/4\) phase \(\mathsf{T}\) one qubit gates, complemented with the two qubits \(\mathsf\CNOT}\) gate. Because of its universality the circuit model is also useful representation of other quantum computation models. Another important class is the topological model, whose first formulation is the toric code of Kitaev.
